Conclusion

Hydrogen gas as a greenhouse gas affects climate change and increases the temperature by one Celsius for every 104.4 ppm of hydrogen. Hydrogen gas is produced from a variety of resources, recycling of the Aluminum industry produces hydrogen gas as a bi-product, for example. Atmospheric hydrogen gas has increased by 70% over the past 150 years. This study used an IoT system to deduce the relation between hydrogen abundance in the atmosphere and increasing temperature. The analysis of the results proved that an increase of hydrogen gas in the atmosphere by 104.4 ppm leads to an increase in temperature by 1ËšC. To decrease hydrogen emissions, Factories should use filters on their chimneys; moreover, it is suggested to reuse the hydrogen that is produced in another field.
